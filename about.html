REVOLVER YOGA

Revolver Yoga Studio’s experienced, highly trained instructors aim to provide the highest possible standard in quality yoga teaching. We want you to have an incredible experience every time you come to class. Whether your goal is to gain strength or flexibility, lose weight, quiet your mind, or learn to relax deeply, our instructors are here to serve you.

If you are newer to yoga and need further guidance about where to start, please contact our staff at any time by sending an email info@revolveryoga.com. Please check the live schedule for details and dates.

NEW STUDENTS

Doing new things is scary, so we want you to feel as prepared for your first yoga class as you can before you even walk in the door.

Clothing

Wear a pair of shorts, capris, or pants that are stretchy and comfortable. A shirt is required for practice, and we recommend wearing something that makes you feel good and isn’t distracting (a shirt you have to tug at all the time? Not fun.). Shoes are not allowed in the studio and you will be asked to remove them at the front desk. Yogis don’t wear socks during practice for safety, so if you really dislike your feet now is the time to confront your fears.

Preparation & Equipment

Bring a mat! If you don’t have one of your own, we rent high quality, 71" Manduka brand mats for a $3 single class fee and also sell Jade and Manduka mats in the studio. It is best to show up to practice well-hydrated and having eaten within the past few hours. Since you’ll be moving around a lot, a big meal right before you come to class isn’t a great idea.

Yoga Etiquette

  • Revolver Yoga Studio is an environment of respect for all within it. Please adhere to these points of etiquette to maximise your own experience as well as that of the community:
  • Studio doors open 20 minutes prior to each class. Please come at least 5 minutes early to check in. New students need to arrive at least 15 minutes early. Class starts promptly at the scheduled time.
  • Cell phone usage is strictly prohibited within the studio. Phones must be turned off or to silent - not vibrate - and left in the tea lounge.
  • Proper Yoga attire includes a shirt or tank top and shorts, pants, or capris. Wear comfortable clothing that moves freely with your body and does not provide distraction to you or others in class.
  • Bring a yoga mat. If you do not have your own, we have high quality Manduka mats available for a $3 one-class rental fee.
  • Shoes are not allowed in the studio. Cubbies are provided for your footwear in the reception area as well as in the tea lounge for your other belongings.

Yoga Myths

At Revolver Yoga Studio, we believe that you can begin the practice of Yoga at any point in your life, regardless of age, sex, athletic ability, or physical shape. What we hear:

I’m too inflexible to practice Yoga.

Unless you are a ballet dancer, gymnast, or in the circus, it’s very normal to feel tight, so don’t worry if touching your toes today seems impossible. The great thing about Yoga is that with regular practice, your flexibility will improve.

I workout at the gym, bike, or run, so I don’t need to practice Yoga.

Although there are many wonderful forms of exercise, Yoga is not only a physical practice and is very effective as a therapeutic system to activate the parasympathetic nervous system (relaxation response), improve chronic illness, and ease mental stress.

Yoga is for girls.

While there are a lot of women practicing Yoga in the U.S. today, Yoga is historically a male-only practice. Many of the most well-respected teachers and practitioners in the world are men! If you think Yoga is going to be too easy, come to one of our Level 2 or 2/3 classes and be ready to sweat.

Yoga is booooooorrrring.

We can understand why you might feel this way. Yoga is more than stretching; it is a series of dynamic movements linked with breath for a full-body, cardiovascular workout which will open the body and the mind. While our Restorative classes are very relaxing, our Level 1/2, Level 2, and Level 2/3 classes are full of exciting mental and physical challenges. No matter which class you attend, you will leave class feeling rejuvenated in both body and mind.

Yoga is a religion.

Yoga is many things to many people. For some, it is purely a physical practice, while for others, Yoga is a state of mind. At Revolver Yoga Studio, we understand the diversity of religious beliefs and respect every person’s right to find their own truth. Our aim is to help you become more comfortable in your body to live a healthy life. We ask that all discussion of religious beliefs and politics please be held at other venues.

I love drinking wine and eating french fries, I can’t possibly give that up!

Here’s the great thing about living in the 21st century...you don’t have to! You can still enjoy a glass of wine or two (or three), eat meat, and practice Yoga. Come as you are.

  • Balanced Mood
  • Weight Management
  • Increased Energy
  • Improved Sleep
  • Reduced Stress
  • Better Focus
  • Decreased Back Pain
  • Total Body Strength

ACTION

Revolver Yoga Studio is deeply committed to the preservation and rebuilding of the environment. In an effort to positively affect the environment, community, and you, we strongly encourage students to ride-share, walk, or bike to class when possible.

As Walla Walla no longer recycles glass, we offer a glass recycling share program. Glass will be collected at the studio and taken to other cities for recycling via our voluntary students and teachers. If you would like to be a part of the program, are headed to a city that recycles glass, or would like to know more about it, please contact courtney@revolveryoga.com.
